WHAT IS TRANSHUMANISM?

“Cyborg” is the contraction of “cybernetic organism”, a term coined in the 1960s by two New York researchers: Manfred Clynes and Nathan S. Kline. It designates the hybridity of a biological organism linked to prostheses. The cyborg is a human grafted with mechanics, electronics. Pop culture is filled with examples of cyborgs: Inspector Gadget, Alita, Iron Man, Darth Vader, Edward Scissorhands… The robot differs from the cyborg in that it is an entirely artificial assembly.

In the 17th century, Ambroise Paré, a surgeon specializing in war wounds, used a wooden sleeve as a prosthesis. Today, it is possible to connect the prosthesis to the nervous system. Another technology: exoskeletons, which are articulated and motorized equipment, attached to certain parts of the body to increase their capacities. Finally, there are implants: artificial devices integrated inside the body that can compensate for a deficient or amputated organ.
In the United States, 42% of healthcare facilities perform preimplantation diagnosis. This expensive process but available on request allows you to choose the color of the eyes, the hair or the sex of the child. In Europe, the European Sperm-Bank promises its customers to find the “ideal” donor according to his personality, his physical characteristics…
In a century, life expectancy has doubled thanks to the drop in infant mortality, the decline in adult mortality and the fight against infections and cardiovascular diseases! On average, life expectancy is 73 years worldwide. The longevity record is held by a Frenchwoman, Jeanne Calment, who died in 1997 at the age of 122! There are more and more centenarians, mainly in developed countries.Indeed, the disparities are strong depending on the country: with an average of 55 years, Sierra Leone has the shortest life expectancy in the world.
